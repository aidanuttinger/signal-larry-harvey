The HFG Repositioning

Introducing Legacy Architecture

HFG has always been a Legacy Architecture firm. This document gives that reality a name, a language, and a strategy for introducing it to the world. You're not changing what you do. You're finally naming it correctly.

Why Category Matters

The Problem With "Wealth Management"

Category is the frame through which every client decision is made. The wrong category positions you against the wrong competitors — and makes your real differentiator invisible.

Current Category
Wealth Management
One of 14,000+ RIAs in the United States. Competing on fee structures, investment performance, and number of services. Every conversation starts with "how are your returns?"

Differentiation: nearly impossible. Commoditized by definition.
→
New Category
Legacy Architecture
A category of one — in Texas, and arguably nationwide. Competing with no one because no one else has claimed it. Every conversation starts with "what do you want your wealth to accomplish?"

Differentiation: immediate, total, and impossible to replicate without 35 years of Larry Harvey's formation.
Category Design Principle
"Don't compete within a category. Design a new one — and become its only member."
Legacy Architecture is not a tagline. It's a category descriptor. It should appear in how HFG is introduced, how the website describes what HFG does, and how Larry describes the firm in every conversation. Repetition creates ownership.
Messaging Framework

What HFG Says — At Every Level

From the one-line positioning to the proof points that support it. Every HFG team member should be able to speak from this framework fluently.

Primary Message
"The wealth you've built deserves to outlast you. We make sure it does."
This is the one line that opens every door. It doesn't speak to features. It speaks to a fear — and a hope — that every wealthy family carries. Use it everywhere.
Category
"HFG is a Legacy Architecture firm — we don't just manage wealth, we build legacies."
The category introduction. Should appear in every bio, every website headline, every introduction Larry makes of HFG. Repetition builds ownership.
Differentiator
"We begin every engagement with the same question: what is this wealth for?"
The process differentiator. Most advisors start with an asset allocation questionnaire. HFG starts with a legacy conversation. That's a different firm for a different client.
Faith Signal
"We believe we are stewards of what's been entrusted to us. That shapes every decision we make."
The values signal. Not preachy. Not pushy. A simple statement of conviction that will resonate immediately with faith-aligned families — and won't repel those who aren't.
Charitable
"Charitable giving isn't a feature we offer. It's a pillar we're built on."
The giving signal. Distinguish HFG from firms that treat philanthropy as a tax strategy. For HFG, it's a conviction. Lead with this to faith-aligned prospects — it's the fastest trust builder you have.

Introduction Strategy

How to Introduce Legacy Architecture

This is a shift — but a gentle one. Clients and colleagues already know you're different. This gives that difference a name.

Internally — Team First
Brief the HFG team on the new category language before anything external changes. Everyone should be able to say: "HFG is a Legacy Architecture firm — we help families build wealth that outlasts them." Consistency is credibility.
Existing Clients — A Letter
A personal letter from Larry: "We've been doing this work for 35 years under one name. We realized we've never fully told you what that name means." Share the manifesto. Invite them to tell you what resonates. This will generate the most powerful referral conversations you've ever had.
New Prospects — Always
From the first conversation: "We're a Legacy Architecture firm — which means we begin every conversation by asking what you want your wealth to accomplish." Watch the room shift. The right prospects will lean forward. The wrong ones will reveal themselves.
The Website — Phase 1
Update the homepage headline and About copy first. Don't redesign everything at once — introduce the language, watch how it performs, then build from there with confidence.
Media & Speaking — Eventually
As the category takes hold, Larry becomes the go-to voice on "Legacy Architecture" — in local media, at conferences, on podcasts. Category creators become category authorities. This is a 12–24 month arc, built intentionally.
