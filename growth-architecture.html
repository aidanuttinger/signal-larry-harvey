The Ideal Client Profile

The Family HFG Was Built For

Not every affluent family is the right HFG client. The right client is the one who makes everything HFG does feel effortless, purposeful, and worth doing.

Who they are
Faith-aligned. Legacy-minded. Values-driven.
They attend church. They give generously. They worry more about what they're leaving behind than what they've accumulated. They want an advisor who speaks their language — not just their financial language, but their values language.
Their wealth profile
$2M–$20M+ in investable assets
Often successful business owners, professionals, or executives. Approaching or at retirement. Beginning to think seriously about wealth transfer and charitable giving. Complex enough to need family-office-quality service.
What they're searching for
An advisor who gets the whole picture
They've had advisors who were great at one thing — investments, or tax, or estate planning — but never all of it. And never with the depth of a relationship that feels like a trusted family advisor, not a service provider.
The disqualifier
Pure performance seekers
Clients who only care about beating the benchmark every quarter are not the right HFG clients. HFG's value isn't in outperforming the S&P. It's in building wealth that outlasts its owner. The right clients understand the difference.

How the Right Family Becomes an HFG Client

Five stages. Each one intentionally designed to deepen trust and build toward the Covenant.

Awareness: They find HFG through a referral, a piece of content, a workshop, or a church community connection. First impression: "This is different."
Trust: They read the manifesto, use the Legacy Impact Calculator, attend a workshop, or consume content. They're not looking for returns — they're looking for resonance.
Conversation: The Discovery Call — Larry or a team member listening deeply, asking the right questions, introducing the Legacy Architecture frame. This is where HFG's signal does the work.
Covenant: The Covenant Meeting — the formal engagement, built around the question "what do you want your wealth to accomplish?" Not an asset allocation discussion. A legacy conversation.
Advocacy: The client who loves HFG refers 2–3 people who are just like them. The referral machine starts. The signal amplifies itself.
